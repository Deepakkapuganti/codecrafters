Steps to make emergency kit

    To be ready for emergencies, whether they are caused by nature or man, or by disasters, it is imperative to put together an emergency kit. The stages to creating an emergency kit are as follows:
  1.  Take into account the demands of your family, including the number of members and any special requirements (such as pets, dietary restrictions, or medical concerns).
  2.  To keep your emergency supplies, use a durable, watertight container. A sizable plastic container, a backpack, or a duffel bag are available options.
  3. Stockpile three days' worth of water, or at least one gallon of water per person every day.
  4. If you need to clean water from natural sources, make sure to pack a portable water filter or purification tablets.
  5. Pack non-perishable food as part of your food supplies.

Hudhud Cyclone

The Hudhud Cyclone was a devastating tropical cyclone that struck the eastern coast of India and Nepal in October 2014. It originated from a low-pressure system in the Bay of Bengal and rapidly intensified into a Category 4 cyclone.
