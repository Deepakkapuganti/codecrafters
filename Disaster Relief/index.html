About Portal

"Disaster Relief" is a comprehensive online platform dedicated to disaster management and mitigation. It offers valuable information and resources for users at every stage of a disaster, including pre-disaster preparedness, during disaster response, and post-disaster recovery. The website provides expert guidance on risk reduction, effective communication strategies for both rescue teams and victims, and a user-friendly map feature that allows users to select specific locations and access critical contact information for assistance. "Disaster Relief" serves as a vital tool for enhancing community resilience and response in the face of natural or man-made disasters.

Natural Hazards

Cyclone

A cyclone is a powerful and rotating storm system characterized by low atmospheric pressure at its center. Cyclones are known by different names in various parts of the world, such as hurricanes in the Atlantic and northeastern Pacific, typhoons in the northwestern Pacific, and cyclones in the Indian Ocean and South Pacific

Tsunami

A tsunami is a powerful and destructive natural disaster that occurs primarily in oceanic and coastal regions. It is typically triggered by events such as underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides, which displace a large volume of water. 

View More >
Earthquakes & Landslides

Earthquakes are sudden and violent shaking of the Earth's surface caused by the release of energy accumulated along geological faults or due to volcanic activity. Landslides, on the other hand, are the rapid downslope movement of rock, soil, and debris due to factors like heavy rainfall, erosion, earthquakes, or volcanic activity

View More >
Floods

Floods are natural disasters that occur when an area becomes inundated with water, typically due to excessive rainfall, snowmelt, storm surges, or the breaching of natural or artificial barriers like dams or levees. Floods can range from localized flash floods to large-scale riverine or coastal flooding events
